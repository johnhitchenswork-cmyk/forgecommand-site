Deep dive 1: who is each one actually built for

This is the decision that matters most, and it is not about which app has more features. It is about which firm you are.

Procore is built for the main contractor with a project controls team, multiple concurrent projects, and a head office that needs financials and document control in one platform. At that scale, the cost and the setup time pay for themselves.

FORGE Command is built for the regional builder, the SME contractor, the subcontractor and the foreman. The person who needs the diary, the snag list and the compliance reference on the phone in their pocket, today, without an implementation project or a per-seat bill. For that firm, Procore is power you pay for and never fully use.

Winner: it depends on your size. Large main contractor, Procore. UK SME builder or foreman, FORGE Command.

Deep dive 2: price over a year

This is where the two genuinely diverge. Procore is an annual, quote-based contract scaled to larger organisations. FORGE Command is £39.99 paid once.

For a small firm, the maths is stark. A per-seat or enterprise platform charges every month for every user, every year, forever. A one-time purchase is paid once and then owned. Over three years, the difference for a small crew is not a rounding error, it is real money that could go into plant, labour or the business.

Winner: FORGE Command for SME budgets. There is no recurring charge to manage, renew or cancel.

Deep dive 3: the daily record and how well it holds up

Both apps record what happens on site. The difference is friction and defensibility.

FORGE Command was designed so the diary builds itself across the day in under two minutes per entry, and every entry is GPS and weather stamped. When a delay or a defect is later disputed, you have the date, the location and the actual weather on record. That turns "I remember it rained" into evidence.

Procore records the same kinds of data inside a much larger system. The capability is there, but the experience is built for a fuller team and more setup, not for one site manager logging quickly with cold hands.

Winner: FORGE Command for fast, defensible on-site recording by a single site manager.
