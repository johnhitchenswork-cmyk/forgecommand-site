How we ranked these apps

Six criteria, weighted toward the realities of a UK site:

  1. Site diary quality, how fast and complete the daily record is
  2. Snagging and defects, capturing, photographing and tracking to sign-off
  3. Compliance fit, CDM awareness, RAMS and method statements, audit trails
  4. On-site usability, does it work with cold hands on a phone, or need a tablet and a desk
  5. Price over a year, one-time versus per-seat-per-month, which adds up fast on a crew
  6. Fit for SME builders, not just enterprise main contractors

2. Procore, the enterprise platform for large main contractors

Procore is the heavyweight. It is a full construction management platform covering project management, quality, safety, financials and more, used by large contractors with the staff to run it.

Key features:

  • Deep project and document management
  • Financials and budget tracking
  • A large integrations marketplace

Pricing: quote-based and aimed at larger budgets, billed annually.

Pros:

  • Extremely broad feature set
  • Strong for multi-project, multi-team head offices

Cons:

  • Priced and built for large contractors, heavy for a small firm
  • Requires real setup and admin time to run well

Best for: large main contractors with a dedicated project controls function.

3. Fieldwire, strong for plans and task management

Fieldwire focuses on drawings, tasks and field coordination, popular with teams that live in their plan markups.

Key features:

  • Plan viewing and markup
  • Task assignment and tracking
  • Punch lists and inspections

Pricing: per user per month, with tiered plans.

Pros:

  • Excellent plan and task handling
  • Works well across a coordinated team

Cons:

  • Per-seat cost grows with the crew
  • Diary and UK compliance are not the core focus

Best for: teams whose day revolves around drawings and task coordination.

4. Buildxact, estimating plus light management

Buildxact leans toward the pre-construction side, estimating and quoting, with project tracking attached. Useful for builders who want the quote and the job in one place.

Key features:

  • Estimating and takeoff
  • Quotes and purchase orders
  • Job scheduling

Pricing: monthly subscription tiers.

Pros:

  • Good for the estimating-to-build handover
  • Cleaner than full enterprise tools

Cons:

  • Site diary and snagging are not the strength
  • Subscription model

Best for: small builders who want estimating and job tracking together.

5. SnagR, enterprise-grade snagging

SnagR is a specialist snagging and quality platform used on larger projects with heavy defect and inspection volumes.

Key features:

  • Detailed defect and inspection workflows
  • Reporting and analytics
  • Plan-based snag location

Pricing: quote-based, enterprise-oriented.

Pros:

  • Very deep on snagging and quality
  • Built for high defect volumes

Cons:

  • Overkill and over-budget for a small firm
  • Narrow focus, not a full site diary tool

Best for: larger projects where snagging volume justifies a dedicated platform.

6. Raken, daily reporting specialist

Raken built its name on fast, clean daily reports and field logs, with time tracking and production data attached.

Key features:

  • Daily reports with photos
  • Time and production tracking
  • Toolbox talks and safety logs

Pricing: per user per month.

Pros:

  • Very good at the daily report
  • Simple field experience

Cons:

  • Per-seat pricing
  • UK-specific compliance is not its main design target

Best for: teams whose priority is a polished daily reporting habit.

7. Re-flow, field workforce management

Re-flow is a UK field management platform aimed at managing mobile workforces, jobs and forms across a fleet of operatives.

Key features:

  • Job and workforce scheduling
  • Digital forms and compliance capture
  • Fleet and field visibility

Pricing: quote-based.

Pros:

  • Strong for managing a dispersed field workforce
  • UK-based

Cons:

  • Broader workforce focus than single-site diary and snagging
  • Quote-based pricing

Best for: firms managing many operatives across many sites.
